## Purpose

DISURI Queens are the brand's distributed content engine. Every post, story, reel, caption, and DM a Queen publishes represents the brand. This framework ensures Queen content stays compliant with FDA/FTC regulations, on-brand with DISURI's voice, and clearly differentiated from MLM marketing — without making the creative process feel like a legal review.

## The Pre-Publish Checklist

Every Queen runs this checklist before publishing any content. Seven questions, each a simple yes/no. All seven must be "Yes" before publishing.

### 1. CLAIMS CHECK: Are all product claims appearance-based only?

**Pass:** "Helps improve the appearance of fine lines and uneven texture"
**Fail:** "Reduces wrinkles," "treats acne," "cures dry skin," "heals damage"

**The rule:** DISURI products are cosmetics, not drugs. Cosmetic claims describe appearance. Drug claims describe treatment, prevention, or cure. Using drug claims without FDA approval is a federal violation.

**Quick test:** Does your sentence use any of these words? If yes, rewrite it.

| Never Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Treats | Helps improve the appearance of |
| Cures | Supports the look of |
| Heals | Helps maintain |
| Repairs (skin) | Helps improve the feel of |
| Reduces (wrinkles/acne) | Helps minimize the appearance of |
| Anti-aging (as a treatment claim) | Helps support a youthful-looking complexion |
| Clears (acne/blemishes) | Helps improve the look of skin clarity |
| Regenerates | Helps revitalize the appearance of |


### 2. INCOME CHECK: Does the content avoid any income claims or recruitment language?

**Pass:** "I love sharing DISURI with my clients" / "Building my beauty business one happy customer at a time"
**Fail:** "Join my team," "Build your downline," "Earn $5K/month," "Financial freedom through DISURI"

**The rule:** FTC requires that income claims be truthful, substantiated, and represent typical results. Since DISURI is in early stages, no income claims should be made unless they reflect the Queen's own documented, verifiable earnings. Never imply income from recruiting other Queens.

**Quick test:** Does your content suggest someone can earn money by joining? If it focuses on recruitment rather than product sales, rewrite it.

| Never Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Join my team | Discover products I love |
| Build your downline | Build your client base |
| Financial freedom | Growing my beauty business |
| Passive income | Earning through products I believe in |
| Ground floor opportunity | Partner with a brand I trust |
| Be your own boss (as income hook) | Share products you genuinely love |


### 3. TERMINOLOGY CHECK: Does the content use approved DISURI language?

**Pass:** "DISURI Queens," "barrier science," "K-beauty authority," "glow"
**Fail:** "Reps," "distributors," "anti-aging miracle," "cure-all"

**Quick reference — Always use:**
- "Queens" (never reps, distributors, sellers, ambassadors)
- "Partner with" (never sign up under, join my team)
- "Barrier science" (never "skin repair science")
- "Helps improve the appearance of" (the universal safe phrasing)
- "Korean-manufactured" (never "Korean-made" — specificity matters)
- "Education-first" (never "sales-first")
- "Glow Like You Own The City" (the brand tagline — use it)
- "Urban luxe" (never "luxury" alone — DISURI is accessible premium)
- Product names exactly as branded: Snail Mucin Cream, Triple Collagen Cream, HA Cream, Foam, Toner, Eye Cream, Essence


### 4. TRANSPARENCY CHECK: Is the content honest and verifiable?

**Pass:** Sharing genuine personal experience with products, disclosing partnership status
**Fail:** Fabricating before/after results, hiding the business relationship, making unsubstantiated ingredient claims

**The rule:** FTC requires disclosure of material connections. If you earn money or receive free products from DISURI, you must disclose this. DISURI's brand is built on radical transparency — hiding the business relationship undermines the brand's core value.

**Required disclosures:**
- Social media posts: #DISURIQueen or #DISURIPartner in caption (not buried in hashtag stacks)
- Video content: Verbal disclosure within the first 30 seconds or persistent on-screen text
- DMs/personal messages: "Full disclosure — I'm a DISURI partner" before product recommendations
- Blog posts/reviews: Clear disclosure at the top, not the bottom


### 5. EDUCATION CHECK: Does the content teach something?

**Pass:** "Multi-weight hyaluronic acid works at three dermal levels — here's why that matters for dehydrated city skin"
**Fail:** "OMG this cream is AMAZING buy it now!! Link in bio!!"

**The rule:** Every piece of DISURI content should leave the viewer knowing something they didn't before. This is what differentiates DISURI from generic beauty brand content. If your post doesn't educate, add a teaching element before publishing.

**Education angles by product:**
- Snail Mucin Cream → Glycoprotein science, moisture barrier function
- Triple Collagen Cream → Multi-collagen peptide types, why topical collagen helps appearance
- HA Cream → Multi-weight HA penetration, city skin dehydration factors
- Foam → pH-balanced cleansing, why barrier preservation matters during cleansing
- Toner → Skin prep science, ingredient absorption optimization
- Eye Cream → Delicate periorbital skin, why eye-specific formulas exist
- Essence → Korean skincare layering philosophy, lightweight hydration science


### 6. TONE CHECK: Does it sound like DISURI?

**Pass:** Warm, confident, specific, like a knowledgeable friend who's genuinely excited
**Fail:** Overly clinical, desperate/salesy, vague/generic, condescending, spa-whisper

**Quick gut check — read your content aloud. Does it sound like:**

| YES (On-Brand) | NO (Off-Brand) |
|----------------|----------------|
| A friend who happens to be really good at her job | A medical professional giving a diagnosis |
| Someone who's genuinely excited to share what she knows | Someone desperately trying to hit a sales target |
| A confident expert having a real conversation | A script being read from a teleprompter |
| An empowered woman building something real | A corporate marketing department |

**Voice keywords to check against:** Warm authority, creative empowerment, radically transparent, education-first, street-smart sophistication


### 7. CULTURAL CHECK: Is the content culturally respectful and inclusive?

**Pass:** Celebrating K-beauty innovation, honoring Latina heritage authentically, showing diverse skin tones and types
**Fail:** Appropriating cultural elements superficially, excluding non-English speakers, stereotyping any community

**The rule:** DISURI sits at the intersection of Korean beauty science and Latina/multicultural entrepreneurship. Content should honor both cultures authentically. Bilingual content (English/Spanish) should give equal weight to both languages, not treat Spanish as an afterthought.

## Common Violations and Fixes

### Violation 1: Accidental Drug Claims

**What happened:** Queen posts "This snail mucin cream HEALS my dry, damaged skin every winter!"

**Why it's a problem:** "Heals" is a drug claim. "Damaged" implies the product repairs a medical condition.

**The fix:** "This snail mucin cream helps my skin look and feel so much more hydrated through winter — the glycoproteins really help improve the appearance of dry, rough texture."


### Violation 2: Implied Income Claims

**What happened:** Queen posts "Quit your 9-5! I make more selling DISURI than I did at my old job!"

**Why it's a problem:** Implies a specific income outcome that new recruits should expect. FTC considers this a "lifestyle claim" that requires substantiation.

**The fix:** "I love that DISURI gives me flexibility to work on my terms. My earnings come from sharing products I genuinely believe in with my clients."


### Violation 3: Missing Disclosure

**What happened:** Queen posts a product review that reads like an organic customer testimonial without mentioning she's a paid partner.

**Why it's a problem:** FTC requires disclosure of material connections. Undisclosed paid endorsements are deceptive advertising.

**The fix:** Add #DISURIPartner to the caption and include "As a DISURI Queen, I partner with this brand..." in the opening.


### Violation 4: Off-Brand Tone

**What happened:** Queen posts "GIRL you NEED this in your life!! Trust me it's literally the BEST thing EVER!!! DM me NOW before we sell out!!!"

**Why it's a problem:** Desperate urgency, no educational value, sounds like every other beauty MLM post — exactly what DISURI is built to counter.

**The fix:** "If your skin is fighting city pollution, humidity, and stress all day — multi-weight hyaluronic acid might be the ingredient you're missing. Here's why I keep DISURI's HA Cream in my daily lineup."

## Enforcement Mechanism

### Level 1: Self-Governance (All Queens)

Every Queen receives this checklist during Queen Academy onboarding (Module 1). It's a personal accountability tool, not a gatekeeping process. Queens self-certify that content passes all 7 checks before publishing.

### Level 2: Peer Review (Recommended for High-Risk Content)

Queens in the private community can tag a "voice champion" or fellow Queen for a quick second-eyes review on high-risk content (business opportunity posts, before/afters, boosted content). This is voluntary but strongly encouraged during the first 90 days.

### Level 3: Brand Review (First 3 Posts + Escalation)

During the Queen pilot program and first 90 days of any new Queen's tenure, the brand team reviews the first 3 pieces of content for each content type. After that, Queens self-govern unless a pattern of violations is flagged.

### Level 4: Corrective Action

If compliance issues are identified:

1. **First occurrence:** Friendly coaching message with specific guidance on what to fix and why
2. **Pattern (3+ occurrences):** One-on-one review session covering the specific compliance area
3. **Persistent issues:** Content must be submitted for pre-approval until compliance is demonstrated
4. **Severe violation** (false medical claims, fabricated income claims, undisclosed material connections): Immediate content removal request + mandatory retraining
