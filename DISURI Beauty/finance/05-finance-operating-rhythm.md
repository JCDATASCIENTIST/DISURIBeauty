# DISURI Beauty -- Finance Operating Rhythm

> **Audit lens:** Accenture (Process and Systems)
>
> **Purpose:** Define the recurring cadence for financial operations so that nothing falls through the cracks. This is the heartbeat of the business's financial discipline.

---

## Operating Calendar Overview

```
DAILY (5 min)
  Review and categorize new Mercury transactions

WEEKLY -- Monday Morning (15 min)
  Cash position check, 13-week plan update, Shopify review

MONTHLY -- 1st Business Day of Month (1-2 hours)
  Full month close, reconciliation, reporting

QUARTERLY -- 1st Week After Quarter End (2-3 hours)
  Sales tax filing, capital review, strategic assessment
```

---

## Daily Checklist (5 Minutes)

Perform every business day, ideally first thing in the morning.

- [ ] Open Mercury -- review any new transactions since yesterday
- [ ] Assign a category code to each uncategorized transaction (see category codes in 01-founder-cash-flow-diagnostic.md)
- [ ] Flag any transaction you do not recognize for investigation
- [ ] If a personal expense was accidentally paid from Mercury, note it for reclassification

**Time target:** Under 5 minutes. If Mercury has no new transactions, skip.

### Step 1: Transaction Reconciliation (30 min)

- [ ] Export Mercury transactions for the prior month (CSV)
- [ ] Verify every transaction has a category code assigned
- [ ] Resolve any flagged or unrecognized transactions
- [ ] Cross-reference Shopify payouts received with Shopify payout reports
- [ ] Verify no personal expenses were paid from Mercury

## Quarterly Review Checklist -- 1st Week After Quarter End (2-3 Hours)

### Tax Compliance

- [ ] Export Shopify tax report for the quarter
- [ ] Calculate total sales tax collected
- [ ] Compare to sales tax reserve balance -- they should match
- [ ] File the quarterly sales-tax return before the deadline
- [ ] Pay the sales tax from the reserve account
- [ ] Document filing confirmation (save screenshot or confirmation number)
- [ ] Review if any new states have triggered economic nexus thresholds

### Quarterly Dates and Deadlines

| Quarter | Period | Sales Tax Due | Est. Income Tax Due |
|---------|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Q1 | Jan 1 - Mar 31 | Apr 30 | Apr 15 |
| Q2 | Apr 1 - Jun 30 | Jul 31 | Jun 15 |
| Q3 | Jul 1 - Sep 30 | Oct 31 | Sep 15 |
| Q4 | Oct 1 - Dec 31 | Jan 31 | Jan 15 |

### Founder Capital Review

- [ ] Total founder capital invested to date: $________
- [ ] Total revenue generated to date: $________
- [ ] Capital efficiency ratio (revenue / capital): ________%
- [ ] Additional capital invested this quarter: $________
- [ ] Is the founder funding cap still appropriate? YES / NO
- [ ] Decision: Continue / Reduce / Increase funding cap

### Inventory Review

- [ ] Current inventory value at cost: $________
- [ ] Units sold this quarter: ________
- [ ] Inventory turnover ratio this quarter: ________
- [ ] Any dead stock (zero sales in 60+ days)? List SKUs:
  - ________________________________________
- [ ] Action plan for dead stock: Markdown / Bundle / Write-off / Hold

### Strategic Assessment (McKinsey Lens)

Answer these questions honestly every quarter:

1. **Is the business closer to break-even than last quarter?** YES / NO
   - Evidence: ________________________________________

2. **Are unit economics positive?** (selling price > COGS per unit) YES / NO
   - Average selling price: $________
   - Average COGS per unit: $________
   - Contribution margin: $________

3. **Is there demonstrated customer demand?** YES / NO
   - Evidence: ________________________________________

4. **Should the business continue, restructure, or wind down?**
   - [ ] Continue -- metrics improving, path visible
   - [ ] Restructure -- cut costs, change approach, narrow focus
   - [ ] Pause -- stop spending, evaluate, decide in 30 days

### Subscription Audit (Repeat from Spending Policy)

- [ ] List all active subscriptions
- [ ] Cancel anything not directly enabling revenue or compliance
- [ ] Document savings from cancelled subscriptions

## Annual Checklist (End of Year)

- [ ] Compile all monthly summaries into an annual financial summary
- [ ] Prepare Schedule C data for personal tax return
- [ ] Calculate total revenue, total expenses, and net income/loss for the year
- [ ] Review total founder capital invested for the year
- [ ] Assess whether the business has met its annual viability goals
- [ ] Set financial targets for the next year
- [ ] File annual state LLC report (if required by state)
- [ ] Renew business license(s) if expiring
