## Executive Summary

DISURI Beauty is a founder-funded product business operating as a single-member LLC. The founder invested $150,000 from personal real estate proceeds to capitalize the business. The business is currently pre-revenue with incomplete bookkeeping, missed quarterly sales-tax filings (with penalties assessed), and no established financial operating cadence.

**Critical findings across all three audit lenses:**

1. **Cash governance is absent.** There are no spending controls, no transaction classification system, and no routine financial review. Personal and business funds have been co-mingled.

2. **Tax compliance is in violation.** Quarterly sales-tax filings have been missed, penalties have been assessed, and there is no workflow connecting Shopify sales-tax collection to state filing obligations.

3. **The $150,000 investment was deployed without a staged approach.** The full amount was invested upfront rather than staged against milestones, creating high burn risk in a pre-revenue business.

4. **There is no financial operating system.** The founder has no regular cadence for reviewing cash, classifying transactions, or making spend decisions against a plan.

**Recommendation:** Implement the operating system defined in this engagement immediately. The business can survive and potentially reach viability, but only with rigorous cash controls and compliance remediation starting now.

### Bain Recommendations

1. **Immediately calculate remaining cash.** The Mercury balance today is the single most important number in the business. Record it.

2. **Reconstruct where the $150,000 went.** Use the Founder Cash-Flow Diagnostic (document 01) to trace every dollar from the original investment to its current state: cash remaining, inventory on hand, money spent and gone.

3. **Establish a 13-week cash plan.** Use document 02. This is non-negotiable. The founder must know, week by week, when the money runs out under realistic assumptions.

4. **Implement spending controls.** Use document 03. Every dollar out must be categorized and justified. Freeze all non-essential spending until revenue exceeds $2,000/month for 2 consecutive months.

5. **Set a founder funding cap.** Decide the absolute maximum personal money to invest. Once that cap is reached, the business must survive on its own revenue or restructure.

## Accenture Audit: Process And Systems

### Findings

| Area | Assessment | Severity |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Transaction classification | None -- transactions are uncategorized | CRITICAL |
| Monthly financial close | Does not exist | CRITICAL |
| Tax compliance workflow | Broken -- filings missed, penalties assessed | CRITICAL |
| Personal/business separation | Incomplete -- co-mingling occurs | HIGH |
| Bookkeeping system | None beyond Mercury's basic transaction log | HIGH |
| Operating cadence | No regular financial review | HIGH |
| Document management | No organized file structure for financial records | MEDIUM |

### Accenture Recommendations

1. **Deploy the transaction classification system.** Every Mercury transaction gets a category code (INV, FUL, SFT, MKT, PRO, INS, TAX, OVR, OCI, ODR, PER). No transaction remains unclassified for more than 48 hours.

2. **Establish the operating rhythm.** Daily (5 min), weekly (15 min), monthly (1-2 hours), quarterly (2-3 hours). Use document 05 as the master checklist.

3. **Fix the tax compliance workflow.** The path from Shopify collecting sales tax to the state receiving a quarterly filing must be documented, tested, and calendared. Use document 04 for the remediation plan.

4. **Set up Mercury account structure.** Main Checking + Tax Reserve vault + Emergency Reserve vault. This physically separates operating cash from tax obligations and emergency funds.

5. **Implement the Cursor AI workflow.** Use document 06. Start with manual CSV exports and AI-assisted classification. Do not automate until the manual process works for 3 consecutive months.

## McKinsey Audit: Strategic Viability

### Findings

| Area | Assessment | Severity |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Business model validation | Not yet tested -- pre-revenue | CRITICAL |
| Capital deployment strategy | Full investment upfront without staging | HIGH |
| Structural overbuild risk | Likely -- $150K is significant for an untested model | HIGH |
| Unit economics | Unknown -- COGS per unit not tracked against price | HIGH |
| Market validation | Unknown -- no sales data to analyze demand | MEDIUM |
| Competitive positioning | Not assessed (requires market data) | MEDIUM |

### McKinsey Recommendations

1. **Separate one-time setup costs from ongoing operating costs.** The $150,000 likely included significant one-time costs (inventory, branding, legal, website). These are sunk. The question going forward is: what are the ongoing monthly costs, and can revenue cover them?

2. **Define unit economics.** For every product:
   - What is the selling price?
   - What is the total cost to produce, package, and ship one unit?
   - What is the contribution margin per unit?
   - How many units per month are needed to cover fixed costs?

3. **Assess inventory position.** If the business bought 12+ months of inventory before proving sales velocity, it is structurally overbought. The priority then shifts from buying more inventory to selling what exists.

4. **Set decision gates.**

| Gate | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Gate 1: Viability test | 90 days from now | If monthly revenue < $500, conduct formal restructure review |
| Gate 2: Sustainability test | 180 days from now | If monthly revenue does not cover monthly fixed costs, reduce scope |
| Gate 3: Capital ceiling | Total invested > $175,000 | Hard stop on new investment. Business must survive on revenue. |

5. **Validate demand before spending.** Before any new inventory, marketing, or product investment:
   - Is there demonstrated customer demand (orders, waitlist, repeat customers)?
   - Can the investment generate 3x return within 90 days?
   - If neither, do not spend.

### McKinsey Strategic Framework: Path to Break-Even

```
Current State ──────────────────────────────────► Break-Even
(Pre-Revenue)                                    (Revenue = OpEx)

Step 1: Know the numbers
  → Complete the cash diagnostic
  → Calculate unit economics
  → Determine monthly fixed costs

Step 2: Prove the unit
  → Sell existing inventory
  → Validate willingness to pay
  → Measure actual COGS per order

Step 3: Reach contribution margin positive
  → Revenue per order > COGS per order
  → This proves the product model works

Step 4: Scale to cover fixed costs
  → Monthly contribution margin > monthly fixed costs
  → This is break-even

Step 5: Generate founder return
  → Monthly profit > 0
  → Begin repaying founder capital
```

## Audit Standard Applied

This engagement applied the rigor of three consulting methodologies:

- **Bain:** "What does the cash say?" -- Focused on near-term liquidity, burn analysis, and capital efficiency. Every recommendation is tied to cash impact.
- **Accenture:** "What process is broken?" -- Focused on controls, workflows, and systems. Every recommendation creates a repeatable, verifiable process.
- **McKinsey:** "Is this business viable?" -- Focused on strategic clarity, unit economics, and decision gates. Every recommendation either proves or disproves the model.

The intersection of all three: **survival requires discipline, and discipline requires a system. This engagement delivers that system.**
