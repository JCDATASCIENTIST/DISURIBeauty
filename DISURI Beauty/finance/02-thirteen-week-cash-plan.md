# DISURI Beauty -- 13-Week Cash Plan

> **Audit lens:** Bain (Cash and Performance)
>
> **Purpose:** Project cash inflows and outflows week by week for the next quarter so the founder can see exactly when money runs out and what levers exist to extend runway.

---

## How to Use This Plan

1. Fill in the starting cash balance from Mercury
2. Enter known fixed costs in the weeks they are due
3. Estimate variable costs conservatively (assume higher than expected)
4. Enter revenue projections conservatively (assume lower than expected)
5. Update actuals weekly every Monday morning
6. If projected ending cash drops below the minimum reserve, take action immediately

**Minimum cash reserve target:** $5,000 (enough to cover 2 weeks of fixed costs plus one tax payment)

## Weekly Update Ritual

Every Monday morning, take 15 minutes:

1. Log in to Mercury -- record current balance
2. Check Shopify -- record last week's orders and payouts
3. Update the cash plan with actuals for the prior week
4. Compare actuals to projections -- note any variance > 10%
5. Look ahead 4 weeks -- flag any week where ending cash drops below $5,000
6. If a shortfall is projected, decide by Tuesday what to cut or defer

## Founder Funding Policy

### Rules for Adding Personal Money to the Business

1. **Document every transfer** as an Owner Contribution with date and amount in the capital reconstruction table (see 01-founder-cash-flow-diagnostic.md)
2. **Set a hard cap** on additional personal investment: $________ total (including the original $150,000)
3. **Never transfer personal money reactively.** Every infusion must be planned at least 1 week in advance and tied to a specific purpose (e.g., "reorder inventory for confirmed demand")
4. **No personal credit cards for business expenses** unless an emergency, documented immediately
5. **Review the total capital invested quarterly.** If total invested exceeds ________ with no revenue traction, trigger a formal restructure review

### Owner Draw Policy

- No owner draws until the business generates positive monthly cash flow for 3 consecutive months
- When draws begin, limit to 25% of net monthly cash flow
- All draws must be documented as ODR transactions in the classification worksheet
