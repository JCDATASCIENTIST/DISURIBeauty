# DISURI Beauty -- Spending Control Policy

> **Audit lens:** Bain (Cash and Performance) + Accenture (Process and Systems)
>
> **Purpose:** Establish hard rules that prevent uncontrolled cash outflow and enforce financial discipline at the founder level.

---

## Core Principle

**Every dollar that leaves the business account must be pre-approved, categorized, and traceable within 48 hours.**

---

## Spending Tiers and Approval Rules

### Tier 1: Auto-Approved (No Review Needed)
- **Threshold:** Under $50 per transaction
- **Examples:** Shipping labels, small supplies, minor subscription charges
- **Requirement:** Must still be categorized within 48 hours in Mercury

### Tier 2: Pre-Planned (Weekly Review)
- **Threshold:** $50 to $500 per transaction
- **Examples:** Packaging reorders, marketing spend, software upgrades
- **Requirement:** Must appear in the 13-week cash plan before being spent. If unplanned, defer to the next weekly review.

### Tier 3: Major (Requires Written Justification)
- **Threshold:** Over $500 per transaction
- **Examples:** Inventory reorders, professional services, equipment
- **Requirement:** Must have a written justification answering:
  1. What is this for?
  2. Why is it needed now?
  3. What happens if it is deferred 30 days?
  4. How does it generate or protect revenue?
  5. Where does the money come from (existing balance or new owner contribution)?

---

## Spending Freeze Categories

The following are **frozen until the business reaches $2,000/month in revenue for 2 consecutive months:**

| Category | Frozen? | Exception Criteria |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| New inventory orders beyond current stock | YES | Only if current stock drops below 30 days of supply at proven sales velocity |
| Paid advertising | YES | Only if a tested channel shows > 3x return on ad spend |
| New software subscriptions | YES | Only if it directly replaces a manual process that takes > 2 hours/week |
| Consultant or agency fees | YES | Only for tax/legal compliance matters |
| Branding or design projects | YES | None until revenue milestone is met |
| Trade shows or events | YES | None until revenue milestone is met |

---

## Personal vs. Business Spending Firewall

### Absolute Rules

1. **All business expenses must be paid from the Mercury business account.** No exceptions without documented reimbursement.
2. **Personal expenses must never be paid from Mercury.** If this happens, reclassify immediately as Owner Draw and document.
3. **Owner contributions (personal to business) require a note in Mercury** with the memo: "Owner Contribution -- [purpose]"
4. **Owner draws (business to personal) are not permitted** until the business is cash-flow positive for 3 consecutive months.

### When Personal Money Must Be Used for Business (Emergency Only)

If a business expense must be paid from personal funds:

1. Pay from personal account
2. Within 24 hours, create a reimbursement entry:
   - Date of purchase
   - Vendor
   - Amount
   - Business purpose
   - Receipt (photo or PDF)
3. Transfer the reimbursement amount from Mercury to personal within 7 days
4. Classify the Mercury transaction as the correct business category (not as Owner Draw)
5. Attach the receipt in Mercury notes

## Inventory Spending Rules

1. **Never reorder inventory on intuition.** Reorder only when:
   - Current stock < 30 days of supply at demonstrated (not projected) sales rate
   - The reorder quantity covers no more than 90 days of projected demand
2. **Track inventory turnover monthly:**
   - Turnover = COGS for the month / average inventory at cost
   - If turnover < 0.5 per month, the business is holding too much stock
3. **Dead stock review:** Any SKU with zero sales in 60 days must be evaluated for markdown, bundle, or write-off

## Spending Review Calendar

| Frequency | Activity | Time Required |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Daily | Categorize any new Mercury transactions | 5 minutes |
| Weekly (Monday) | Review 13-week cash plan, update actuals, flag variances | 15 minutes |
| Monthly (1st of month) | Full monthly close, subscription audit, spending report | 1 hour |
| Quarterly | Tax filing, inventory review, founder capital review, freeze assessment | 2 hours |

---

## Violation Protocol

If a spending rule is violated:

1. Document what happened and why within 24 hours
2. Reclassify the transaction correctly in Mercury
3. Determine root cause: Was it an emergency, a process gap, or a discipline failure?
4. If it is a pattern (3+ violations in a quarter), reassess whether the spending controls need to be tightened or whether the underlying issue is a cash management problem
