# DISURI Beauty: Founder Voice vs. Brand Voice Transition Plan

**Created:** March 7, 2026
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active — Phase 1 (Founder-Led)
**Source Documents:** Brand Strategy v3.0, Queens Program Framework, Brand Style Guide

---

## Why This Matters

DISURI Beauty's voice and Joel's voice are currently the same thing. That's not a weakness — it's a deliberate startup-phase asset. The founder's credibility (corporate marketing career, Amazon failure story, professional product sourcing) gives the brand authenticity that competitors can't replicate.

The risk isn't that the founder voice exists. The risk is that as Queens begin generating 60–80% of brand content, the voice either fragments (every Queen sounds different) or dilutes (nobody sounds like the brand anymore). This plan defines what stays constant, what transitions, and when.

## The Voice Architecture

### Permanent Brand Elements (Never Transfer Away)

These are brand DNA — they belong to DISURI regardless of who's speaking. Every piece of content from any source must carry these:

| Element | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Radical Transparency | Publish what's in the bottle, publish what partners earn | "We list every ingredient concentration. Not because we have to. Because you deserve to know." |
| Education-First Positioning | Teach before you sell; every post should leave someone smarter | "Multi-weight hyaluronic acid penetrates at three dermal levels. Here's why single-weight HA isn't enough." |
| Anti-MLM Immune System | Never imply income from recruiting; earnings come from product sales only | "You earn when customers buy. Period. No downlines, no team-building quotas." |
| Appearance-Based Claims Only | FDA/FTC compliant language — "helps improve the appearance of," never "treats/cures/heals" | "Helps improve the appearance of fine lines" — never "reduces wrinkles" |
| Confident Authority Without Arrogance | Know your science; don't lecture about it | "Snail mucin glycoproteins support your skin's moisture barrier. Want to know why that matters for city skin?" |
| Bilingual Authenticity | English/Spanish co-equal, never as an afterthought | Primary copy in English, Spanish translation with equal visual weight |

### Founder-Specific Elements (Phase 1 Only)

These elements are powerful now but should gradually become "brand origin story" rather than active content drivers:

| Element | Current Role | Transition Path |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Amazon Failure Story | Primary credibility builder — "I lost money on Amazon so you don't have to" | Becomes brand origin mythology in the "About" page; Queens reference it as "our founder's story" rather than speaking it as their own |
| Corporate Marketing Career References | Establishes Joel as a pro, not a hobbyist | Transitions to "DISURI is built by professional marketers" — institutional credibility replaces personal credibility |
| Joel's Personal Product Testing | "I tested 200+ formulations before choosing these" | Becomes "Our team evaluates hundreds of formulations" — the rigor stays, the personal pronoun shifts |
| Direct-to-Camera Founder Content | Joel as the face of the brand in video/social | Queens become the distributed face; Joel appears for milestone moments (launches, annual updates, community events) |
| First-Person Vulnerability | "I didn't know if this would work" | Queens tell their own vulnerability stories using the same tone framework, not Joel's specific stories |

## Phase Model

### Phase 1: Founder-Led (Current — Q1-Q2 2026)

**Trigger to enter:** Brand launch through first 30-day Queen pilot

**Voice ratio:** 80% Founder / 20% Queens (using templates)

**What this looks like:**
- Joel creates all anchor content (brand videos, product launches, email campaigns)
- Queens use the AI Prompt Library and Content Calendar templates to create supporting content
- All Queen content follows the Tone-by-Context Matrix from the brand voice guidelines
- Joel reviews/approves Queen content during pilot program

**Success metric:** 10-20 founding Queens producing on-brand content using the toolkit without requiring heavy editing


### Phase 2: Co-Voice (Q3-Q4 2026)

**Trigger to enter:** Queens producing content that passes the Content Compliance Checklist (see companion document) with <15% revision rate

**Voice ratio:** 50% Founder / 50% Queens

**What this looks like:**
- Queens create content independently using established frameworks
- Joel creates strategic/milestone content (product launches, brand partnerships, quarterly updates)
- Queen "voice champions" emerge — top performers whose content becomes exemplar material
- Brand voice guidelines are updated with real Queen content examples (replacing prescribed-only examples)

**Success metric:** At least 5 Queens consistently producing content that scores 4/5+ on brand voice alignment without review


### Phase 3: Brand-Led (2027+)

**Trigger to enter:** Queen-generated content outperforms founder content in engagement metrics; 10+ Queens consistently on-brand

**Voice ratio:** 20% Founder / 80% Queens + Brand team

**What this looks like:**
- The brand voice is fully institutional — it belongs to DISURI, not Joel
- Joel's role shifts to "founder emeritus" content: vision pieces, community celebration, milestone moments
- Queens are the primary content engine; their authentic stories replace the founder origin story as the brand's proof points
- New Queens learn voice from the guidelines + exemplar Queen content, not from mimicking Joel
- The AI marketing toolkit generates content that sounds like "DISURI" without sounding like "Joel"

**Success metric:** Brand voice consistency score of 90%+ across all content sources; new Queens onboard to voice in <2 weeks

## The Handoff Checklist

When moving between phases, verify these before progressing:

### Phase 1 → Phase 2

- [ ] At least 10 Queens have completed Queen Academy Module 1 (Brand Foundation)
- [ ] Queen content revision rate is below 15%
- [ ] 3+ Queen content pieces have been added to the "Content Examples" section of the guidelines
- [ ] Queens can articulate the "We Are / We Are Not" table without referencing it
- [ ] No FDA/FTC compliance violations in Queen content during pilot

### Phase 2 → Phase 3

- [ ] 5+ Queens identified as "voice champions" with consistently on-brand content
- [ ] Brand voice guidelines updated with data-backed "Language That Works" section (performance metrics, not just prescriptions)
- [ ] Queen-generated content achieves equal or higher engagement than founder content
- [ ] New Queens successfully onboard to voice using guidelines alone (without direct founder coaching)
- [ ] Founder origin story has been formalized into institutional "About DISURI" narrative
- [ ] Content Compliance Checklist pass rate is 95%+ across all Queens

## What Queens Should Never Do

Regardless of phase, Queens should never:

1. Tell Joel's personal stories as if they're their own (the Amazon failure, the corporate marketing career, the product testing journey)
2. Speak as the brand founder — they speak as brand partners
3. Make claims that exceed "appearance-based" language
4. Reference income potential beyond their own documented earnings
5. Create content that positions DISURI as an MLM or network marketing opportunity

Queens should always:

1. Tell their own authentic stories using DISURI's voice framework
2. Reference "DISURI" or "our brand" rather than "I created this" or "my company"
3. Use the approved terminology from the brand voice guidelines
4. Run content through the Content Compliance Checklist before publishing
5. Credit the founder story accurately when referencing brand origins: "Joel built DISURI after..." not "I built DISURI..."

## Measuring Voice Consistency

### Quarterly Voice Audit (Starting Phase 2)

Sample 20 pieces of content (10 founder, 10 Queen) and score each on:

| Dimension | 1 (Off-Brand) | 3 (Acceptable) | 5 (Exemplary) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| Transparency | Vague claims, hidden info | Honest but not proactively transparent | Proactively shares ingredients, process, earnings |
| Education Value | Pure sales pitch | Mentions a benefit | Teaches something specific the reader can use |
| Anti-MLM Clarity | Implies recruitment income | Neutral on business model | Explicitly product-sales-only language |
| Compliance | Uses prohibited terms | Mostly compliant | Flawless appearance-based language |
| Tone Match | Doesn't sound like DISURI | Recognizable but inconsistent | Indistinguishable from brand anchor content |

**Target:** Average score of 4.0+ across all dimensions by end of Phase 2.

## Integration with Brand Voice Guidelines

This transition plan supplements the DISURI Beauty Brand Voice Guidelines (v2). The "Voice Attributes," "Tone-by-Context Matrix," and "Terminology Guide" sections of the guidelines are the shared foundation that all content creators — founder and Queens alike — must follow.

The transition plan addresses *who* speaks and *when*, while the guidelines address *how* everyone speaks.
