## Executive Summary

DISURI Beauty is a Korean-manufactured skincare brand sold through a partner network of trained micro-entrepreneurs called "Queens." The brand voice lives at the intersection of **warm authority** and **creative empowerment** — described internally as "the friend who happens to be really, really good at her job." She's knowledgeable and specific without being clinical, confident without being arrogant, and honest without being harsh.

What makes DISURI distinctive is its dual audience: end consumers who want ingredient-transparent K-beauty, and entrepreneurial partners who want real marketing skills without MLM exploitation. The voice must simultaneously educate consumers on verifiable skincare science (Sage archetype) and inspire Queens to build their own businesses (Creator archetype). Every piece of content should feel like it's teaching you something valuable — whether that's why multi-weight hyaluronic acid matters or how to write a caption that converts.

The brand's north star is radical transparency: publish what's in the bottle, publish what partners earn, no fine print. This transparency is not a marketing tactic — it is the brand's immune system against MLM stigma and the beauty industry's credibility crisis.

## We Are / We Are Not

The foundational brand identity anchor. Voice is constant — it doesn't change by channel or audience.

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| **Warm** — Friendly, approachable, genuine; we talk to people like equals | **Bubbly or performative** — No "OMG babe you NEED this!! 😍😍😍"; no forced excitement or hyperbolic enthusiasm |
| **Authoritative** — Knowledgeable, specific, evidence-based; we cite concentrations and data | **Vague or hand-wavy** — No "proprietary blends" or "finest ingredients"; we never hide behind ambiguity |
| **Confident** — Self-assured, no hedging, no unnecessary apologies | **Arrogant or dismissive** — We don't talk down to competitors' customers or make absolute superiority claims |
| **Honest** — Direct, transparent, unafraid of hard truths including our own failures | **Salesy or manipulative** — No urgency tactics, no countdown timers, no "last chance" pressure language |
| **Empowering** — Encouraging without being patronizing; we give tools, not dependencies | **Patronizing or guru-like** — No "I'll show you the secret to changing your life forever"; no savior complex |
| **Scientific** — We ground every claim in verifiable data (ppm, pH, molecular weights) | **Clinical or cold** — We present science through a warm, human lens, not sterile lab language |
| **Community-oriented** — We celebrate Queens and peers, not the brand itself | **Self-congratulatory** — DISURI is the platform; Queens are the stars; we don't center ourselves |

### Voice Attributes Detail

#### Attribute 1: Warm Authority
- **What it means**: We combine genuine friendliness with deep product and marketing knowledge. We sound like a knowledgeable friend sharing what she knows, not a brand broadcasting at an audience.
- **How it shows up**: Conversational sentence structures with specific data points woven in naturally. "Let me show you exactly what's in this and why I love it" not "Our advanced formulation delivers results."
- **What to avoid**: Excessive exclamation marks, emoji overuse, over-the-top enthusiasm, or alternatively, dry/clinical tone stripped of personality.
- **Evidence**: Brand strategy voice identity section: "DISURI's voice is the friend who happens to be really, really good at her job. She's warm but not bubbly. Confident but not arrogant. Smart but never condescending."
- **Confidence**: High

#### Attribute 2: Radical Transparency
- **What it means**: We publish specific, verifiable data — ingredient concentrations in ppm, partner economics with real margins, and our own failure story. We don't just claim transparency; we operationalize it.
- **How it shows up**: Citing "92% snail secretion filtrate at 1,000 ppm" instead of "high concentration." Publishing wholesale margins (50%) instead of "great earning potential." Sharing the $11K Amazon loss openly.
- **What to avoid**: Proprietary blend language, vague ingredient claims, income promises, "trust us" appeals without evidence.
- **Evidence**: Core value TRANSPARENCY: "We publish what's in the bottle and what partners earn. No hidden formulas. No hidden fees. No fine print." Product truth table with specific ppm for all SKUs.
- **Confidence**: High

#### Attribute 3: Education-First
- **What it means**: We teach rather than sell. When we explain why something matters (multi-weight HA, pH-balanced cleansing, content hooks), the purchase or partnership decision follows naturally.
- **How it shows up**: Content that explains the "why" before the "buy." Academy curriculum references. Ingredient science explanations. Marketing skill sharing in Skool community.
- **What to avoid**: Hard sells, product pushing without context, "just buy it" energy, persuasion without education.
- **Evidence**: Experience Principle: "Education Over Persuasion: Teach, don't sell. When a Queen explains why multi-weight hyaluronic acid matters, the sale follows naturally."
- **Confidence**: High

#### Attribute 4: Anti-MLM Integrity
- **What it means**: We are acutely aware of MLM stigma and proactively distance ourselves through language, structure, and transparency. This is both a brand value and a legal compliance requirement.
- **How it shows up**: Using "partner" / "Queen" / "affiliate" never "distributor" / "rep" / "downline." Saying "earn commissions on sales you drive" never "make money" / "financial freedom." No income claims. Free entry tier.
- **What to avoid**: ANY language that sounds MLM-adjacent — income promises, recruitment pressure, "opportunity" framing, upline/downline references, "proven system" language.
- **Evidence**: Language Rules table with explicit Always Say / Never Say pairs. Partner persona's core fear: "Being seen as 'one of those MLM people.'" Model differentiation layer built on non-exploitation.
- **Confidence**: High

#### Attribute 5: Aspirational Accessibility
- **What it means**: We position the brand as premium but reachable — both in product pricing ($15-45) and in entrepreneurial entry (free Affiliate tier). Ambition without exclusivity.
- **How it shows up**: "Premium Korean skincare without the luxury tax." "Start as a free Affiliate. No kit to buy." Tagline "Glow Like You Own The City" — urban confidence, not elitism.
- **What to avoid**: Luxury-exclusive positioning, gatekeeping language, making products or partnership feel out of reach, or conversely, discounting language that undermines premium perception.
- **Evidence**: Brand adjective "Accessible": "Premium quality at fair prices; expertise shared freely." Positioning as "accessible premium" in competitive landscape.
- **Confidence**: High

## Brand Personality

- **Archetypes**: Primary — **The Sage** (truth, knowledge, education); Secondary — **The Creator** (building, expression, tools)
- **If our brand were a person**: A 30-year-old professional digital marketer who moonlights as a skincare educator on TikTok. She has a corporate day job managing real campaigns, so she has genuine expertise — not guru energy. She's the one in the friend group everyone asks for skincare advice AND career advice. She failed publicly (Amazon loss), rebuilt transparently, and now teaches what she learned. She celebrates her friends' wins louder than her own.
- **Core values expressed in voice**: Transparency, Empowerment, Rigor, Community, Resilience
- **Archetype tension**: The Sage × Creator intersection means we balance rigorous science with creative entrepreneurial energy. We avoid being either too clinical (The Ordinary) or too hype-driven (early Glossier). We teach the truth AND give you tools to build from it.

### Logo Usage

**Three logo formats available:**
1. **Primary Logo** — Stacked icon (silhouette in square frame) with "DISURI BEAUTY" wordmark below. Available in: full color (black + Power Crimson accent), monochrome black, reversed white on black
2. **Secondary Logo** — Horizontal icon + wordmark side by side. Same color variants
3. **Stacked Name** — "DISURI BEAUTY" wordmark only (no icon). For tight spaces

**Logo rules:**
- Never scale, warp, recolor, or add effects to the logo
- Never use gradients or unapproved color hues on the logo
- Maintain clear space around logo equal to the height of the "D" icon
- On dark backgrounds, use reversed (white) version — never place dark logo on dark backgrounds

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Value Proposition
Korean skincare science meets entrepreneurial ambition — verified ingredients combined with professional digital marketing education.

Variations:
- "For ambitious women who want effective skincare and the skills to build something of their own" (positioning statement)
- "We don't just make skincare. We make skincare entrepreneurs. And we give them the science to back up every word." (brand manifesto)
- "So you don't just glow, you grow." (tagline extension)

### Key Message Pillars

1. **Ingredient Transparency (B2C-primary)**
   - Core idea: Every product claim is backed by published ppm concentrations and Korean manufacturing provenance
   - When to use: Product content, consumer-facing materials, comparison content, objection handling around "what's actually in it"
   - Example phrasing: "Our Snail Mucin Cream contains 92% snail secretion filtrate at 1,000 ppm. We don't just say it works — we show you exactly what's inside."
   - Confidence: High

2. **Skills Over Schemes (B2B-primary)**
   - Core idea: We teach professional digital marketing skills with standalone career value — not "how to sell our products"
   - When to use: Recruitment content, Queen onboarding, partner retention, differentiation from MLM competitors
   - Example phrasing: "We don't teach you to recruit. We teach you to market. Digital marketing skills you'll use whether you sell DISURI for a month or a decade."
   - Confidence: High

3. **Community Without Exploitation (Both)**
   - Core idea: A partner network built on peer support, fair margins, and zero required purchases — the anti-MLM
   - When to use: Addressing MLM skepticism, explaining the business model, building trust with both consumers and prospective Queens
   - Example phrasing: "Your success doesn't come from recruiting people beneath you. It comes from selling products people love and building an audience that trusts you."
   - Confidence: High

4. **K-Beauty Heritage (B2C-primary)**
   - Core idea: Manufactured in South Korea with pharmaceutical-grade processes — real K-beauty provenance, not just marketing
   - When to use: Product positioning, credibility building, differentiating from Western brands with K-beauty-inspired lines
   - Example phrasing: "Formulated and manufactured in South Korea using the same pharmaceutical-grade processes behind the world's most trusted skincare."
   - Confidence: High

5. **Zero-Risk Entry (B2B-primary)**
   - Core idea: Free Affiliate tier eliminates the financial barrier that makes MLM predatory
   - When to use: Overcoming partnership hesitation, addressing "Maria's" core fear of financial loss
   - Example phrasing: "Start as a free Affiliate. No kit to buy. No inventory to hold. Just your link, your content, and your commission."
   - Confidence: High

### Competitive Positioning
- vs. **COSRX / Beauty of Joseon** (mass K-beauty): We add education, community, and verified ppm transparency to K-beauty; they're pure product plays vulnerable to commoditization
- vs. **Peach & Lily / Glow Recipe** (premium K-beauty): We offer partner economics and accessible pricing; they rely on traditional DTC/retail with higher price points and no community model
- vs. **Rodan + Fields / Arbonne** (MLM): We offer transparent wholesale margins, free entry, no recruitment pressure, and genuine skills training; they exploit with high buy-in, multi-level commissions, and income claims
- vs. **Status Quo** (consumer doing nothing): Your skin deserves ingredients you can verify; your side-hustle ambitions deserve a model that doesn't exploit you

## Tone-by-Context Matrix (Confidence: High)

Voice is constant. Tone flexes by context. These three dimensions adjust:

| Context | Formality | Energy | Technical Depth | Key Principle |
|---------|-----------|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Instagram Feed | Medium | Medium-High | Medium | Polished + educational; lead with ingredient science |
| TikTok | Low | High | Low-Medium | Casual + direct + fast; hook-first in 3 seconds |

| Queen Training | Medium | Warm | High | Instructional + encouraging; practical and specific |
| Customer Service | Medium | Warm | Low-Medium | Empathetic + competent; solve fast |

### Context-Specific Guidelines

#### Instagram Feed
- **Overall tone**: Polished educator — beautiful imagery paired with real information
- **Opening approach**: Lead with an ingredient fact or skin science insight, not a product pitch
- **Do's**: Use swipe-through carousels for ingredient education; cite specific ppm; include clear CTAs; spotlight Queens
- **Don'ts**: Over-filter; use generic beauty language; post without educational value; use pushy sales language
- **Example**: "Your skin's moisture barrier needs more than hype. Triple-weight HA means hydration at every layer. Swipe to see how it works →"

#### TikTok
- **Overall tone**: Fast, confident, hook-driven — get to the point in the first 3 seconds
- **Opening approach**: Lead with a surprising fact, product comparison, or visual demo
- **Do's**: Show product texture on skin; cite specific numbers; keep under 60 seconds; use trending sounds strategically
- **Don'ts**: Over-produce; use long intros; be vague about ingredients; use MLM-coded language
- **Example**: "92% snail mucin. In a cream. Not an essence. Let me show you why that matters in 30 seconds."

#### Live Selling (Instagram/TikTok)
- **Overall tone**: Real-time conversation — demo-focused, responsive, authentic
- **Opening approach**: Start applying product immediately; narrate what you're doing and why
- **Do's**: Answer questions in real-time; show texture and application; share personal experience; create community energy
- **Don'ts**: Read from scripts; ignore questions; use high-pressure closing tactics; make medical claims
- **Example**: "Okay so this is the Snail Mucin Cream — watch what happens when I put it on. See that glow? That's the snail secretion doing its thing."

#### Recruitment Content
- **Overall tone**: Vulnerable and credible — share the real journey, including failures
- **Opening approach**: Lead with the founder's origin story or a Queen's real experience
- **Do's**: Share the Amazon failure story; explain the free entry tier; emphasize skills that transfer; show community
- **Don'ts**: Make income claims; use "opportunity" language; create urgency; promise lifestyle transformation
- **Example**: "I failed publicly on Amazon. I'm rebuilding with everything I learned. If you want the same skills, here's how to start — for free."

#### Skool Community
- **Overall tone**: Generous mentor and peer — share playbooks, templates, and real results
- **Opening approach**: Lead with value — share a template, a result, or a lesson learned
- **Do's**: Share exact caption templates; give platform-specific advice; celebrate peer wins; be generous with knowledge
- **Don'ts**: Gatekeep information; compete with other Queens; be vague; withhold strategies
- **Example**: "Here's the exact caption template I used for my top-performing post last week. Take it, change the hook, make it yours."

#### Email / Shopify
- **Overall tone**: Warm professional — benefit-driven with evidence
- **Opening approach**: Lead with a skin benefit or routine recommendation
- **Do's**: Include ppm proof points; suggest routines; use clear CTAs; personalize when possible
- **Don'ts**: Use aggressive subject lines; oversend; make medical claims; use countdown urgency
- **Example**: "Your skin deserves Korean science, not marketing guesswork. Here's your 3-step routine — with the ppm to prove it."

## Language That Works (Confidence: Medium)

*Note: No conversation transcripts were analyzed. These examples are derived from the brand strategy document's messaging architecture and channel-specific examples. Effectiveness ratings are based on strategic intent, not measured performance data.*

### Top Phrases
1. **"We don't just say it works — we show you exactly what's inside."** — Context: Consumer-facing product content. Why it works: Directly addresses skepticism about beauty industry opacity; positions DISURI as the antidote.
2. **"We don't teach you to recruit. We teach you to market."** — Context: Queen recruitment. Why it works: Immediately differentiates from MLM in one sentence; speaks to Maria's core fear.
3. **"I lost $11K on Amazon. Here's what I learned and how I rebuilt."** — Context: Origin story / recruitment. Why it works: Vulnerability builds credibility; shows resilience; makes the founder human.
4. **"You already have what it takes. I just have the tools."** — Context: Queen encouragement. Why it works: Empowers without patronizing; positions DISURI as enabler, not savior.
5. **"Glow Like You Own The City."** — Context: Brand tagline across all touchpoints. Why it works: Dual meaning — own the glow (B2C) and own the city/market (B2B); aspirational without being exclusive.

### Questions That Engage
1. **"Do you know what's actually in your skincare?"** — Leads to: Ingredient transparency conversation; positions DISURI as the brand with answers
2. **"What if the skills you learned selling skincare were skills you could put on a resume?"** — Leads to: Skills-over-schemes conversation; differentiates from MLM "opportunity" pitch

### Objection Handling Patterns
1. **Objection**: "Isn't this just another MLM?"
   **Response pattern**: "I totally understand that concern. Here's what's different: there's no buy-in required (free Affiliate tier), no recruitment commissions (you earn on your own sales), and the marketing skills you learn are yours to keep forever — whether you sell DISURI or not."
   **Why it works**: Acknowledges the fear (Maria's core fear), then systematically dismantles it with specific structural differences.

2. **Objection**: "Why should I pay more than drugstore skincare?"
   **Response pattern**: "Great question. Our Snail Mucin Cream has 92% snail secretion filtrate at 1,000 ppm — we publish that number. Most drugstore brands won't tell you their concentrations because they'd have to admit how little active ingredient is actually in the bottle."
   **Why it works**: Shifts from price comparison to value/transparency comparison; uses specific data to reframe.

### Examples to Avoid (Off-Brand)

**Off-Brand Consumer Post:**
> "OMG babe you NEED this!! 😍😍😍 This cream is AMAZING and will literally change your life!!! DM me for deets!!"

*What's wrong:* Performative enthusiasm violates Warm-not-Bubbly principle. "Change your life" is guru language. "DM me for deets" is MLM-coded. Zero educational value. Emoji overuse.

**Off-Brand Recruitment:**
> "Ready for financial freedom? 💰 Join my team and start your journey to being your own boss! This proven system generates passive income while you sleep!"

*What's wrong:* "Financial freedom" is a Never-Use term (FTC). "Proven system" is prohibited. "Passive income" implies no effort. "Join my team" uses MLM recruitment framing. Income claims without IDS.

**Off-Brand Product Description:**
> "Our proprietary blend of the finest ingredients fights aging and erases wrinkles for a miraculous transformation."

*What's wrong:* "Proprietary blend" contradicts transparency. "Finest ingredients" is vague. "Fights aging" / "erases wrinkles" / "miraculous" are FDA-noncompliant medical claims.
